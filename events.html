    • Kannada Rajyotsava



    The Kannada Rajyotsava celebration at BMSCCM,with a lot enthusiasm and ardour. No festival is complete without colourful and vibrant festivities. The kannada anikethana sangha responsibility of making .this event a successful one and with all the dedication of our seniors, volunteers, the support of our dearest Principal and sta members, this event was a great success.

    No winter lasts forever, no spring skips its turn. In the same way, BMS never skips celebrating the culture and heritage of our country. With a lot of enthusiasm, everyone dressed in traditional aire with the dress code being red or yellow. The seminar hall was also decorated in red and yellow in keeping with the colours of the flag of Karnataka. Dr. Pankaj Choudhary rendered the welcome speech in Kannada, which made all of us stunned and happy to hear him speak in Kannada and made all the chief guests feel welcome. The event began by welcoming the chief guests, followed by a patriotic song in Kannada by a group which brought out a feeling of pride of living in Karnataka.

    We had Padma Shri awardee, Matha B Manjamma Jogati who is a transgender folk dancer of Jogamma heritage and also the rst transgender president of the Karnataka Transgender Janapada Academy. She was honoured with a Padma Shri Award for her hard work and dedication in keeping the ancient Jogati tradition alive and her other contributions to the preservation of the Janapada culture. It was such a privilege to have her at BMSCCM for this event. Our Principal and other staff members honoured all the chief guests and the event began by lighting the lamp with a beautiful song.

    • MINDTRICK

    BMSCCM at VV Puram , Bangalore organized an All India Chess Tournament, Mindtrick 2022 Intercollege chess competition, in association with Mandya Chess Academy on Tuesday. Dr.Pankkaj Choudhary, principal of BMSCCM welcomed chief guest Master Pranav V, youngest international chess master of India and Arvind Shastri, CEO KSCA and National 'A' Player the other dignitaries who shared the dias were Aviram Sharma, Chairman BMSCCM and trustee BMS Educational Trust, BS Sanjeeva Director Finance BMSET and Madhuri from Mandya Chess Academy. There were about 258 participants from across the country. The tournament had a cash prize of 50,000. Prajwal VP from St Claret College, Bengaluru emerged as the champion and took home a trophy and cash prize of Rs.25,000.

    • SAMSKRUTHI


    A two day inter-collegiate fest Samskruthi starts with big pomp ,The fest is jointly organised by BMS College of Commerce and Management, V V Puram, Bengaluru and BMS Evening College of Arts and Commerce, Basavanagudi, Bengaluru . The fest is dedicated to “FIT INDIA MOVEMENT – A STEP TOWARDS HEALTHY INDIA”. To support the cause Zumba and fitness class was organised for students from 7-8 AM in the morning. The intercollegiate fest Samskruthi was inaugurated by Vidya Balan, Indian Film Actress, Miss Shanvi Srivatsava, Indian film actress . The Guests of Honor were - Dr. B.S Ragini Narayan, Donor Trustee, BMS Educational Trust, Sri. M. Madan Gopal, IAS(Retd.) Trustee BMSET, Sri Aviram Sharma, Chairman, BMSCCM and Prof. Pankaj Choudhary, Principal, BMSCCM & BMSECAC. The evening program also witnessed an amazing playback singer– Usha Uthup followed by DJ Night in the college ground.

    • NSS CAMP

    NSS is an active program run by the Ministry of Sports, Government of India. The students participate in its activities work with the people of society in the interest of society. The N.S.S. (National Service Scheme) Unit of BMS College, organized a camp on 10/03/2024 to 16/03/2024 at Anugondanahalli for 7 days. 50 volunteers were taken part in the camp activities. The program was inaugurated by Govinda Gowda, NSS co-ordinator (BCU). All the volunteer participated with full enthusiasm and zeal in all the activities which was assigned by program head. They cleaned the govt school premises, maintained t he road way s, paint ed t he government schools’ walls and spread awareness of the right to education and abolition of child marriage in the village and they had done the survey on the social problems faced by the villagers, at last camp ended with nice valedictory programme.
